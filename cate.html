Spaghetti Pasta Carbonara

  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 20 minutes
  • Serving size: 4-6 portions

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp olive oil or unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound thick cut bacon, diced
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced, about 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • 3-4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated parmesan or pecorino cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti pasta (or be creative, and substitute other pasta)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Heat pasta water: In a large pot, boil 4 quarts of water. Add one to two tablespoons of salt to the cooking water.

  2. Sauté bacon and garlic: While the water is heating, heat the olive oil in a large sauté pan over medium heat. When the oil is hot, add the bacon and reduce heat so it cooks slowly, until crispy. Add the garlic (if using) and cook another minute. Turn off the heat, remove from pan and place bacon and garlic mixture into a large bowl.

  3. Beat eggs and add half the cheese: In a small bowl, lightly beat the eggs and mix in about half the cheese.

  4. Cook pasta: Once the water has reached a rolling boil, add the dry pasta, and cook, uncovered, at a rolling boil.

  5. Toss pasta with bacon: When the pasta is al dente (slightly firm), use tongs to move it to the bowl with the bacon and garlic, or drain lightly so it is still dripping wet. Save some of the pasta water.

    Move quickly, because you want the pasta to be hot. The heat of the pasta will heat the eggs to create a creamy sauce. Toss everything to combine, allowing the pasta to cool slightly.

  6. Add the beaten egg mixture: Add the beaten eggs with cheese and toss quickly to combine once more. Add salt to taste. Add some pasta water back to the pasta to keep it from drying out.

  7. Serve at once with the rest of the parmesan and freshly ground black pepper. You can add some freshly chopped parsley for taste and visual appeal.
